MP1 PRODUCT/SERVICE - SIMULATION GUIDE (20 PTS COMPLETE TIER)
======================================================================

Below are the step-by-step instructions on how to perfectly simulate and 
test all four rubric requirements in your application.

REQUIREMENT 1: Product/Service CRUD datatable (8pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Navigate to your admin dashboard and click on "Products" in the sidebar 
   (/admin/products).
2. To test CREATE: Click the "Add Product" button. Fill in a test name like 
   "Simulation Laptop", set a price, stock quantity, category, and brand. 
   Click "Save".
3. To test READ & DATATABLE: Go back to the product list. Notice that the 
   table has a search bar at the top right, and pagination at the bottom right. 
   Search for "Simulation Laptop". The table will instantly filter the list 
   without reloading the page.
4. To test UPDATE: Click the blue Edit icon (pencil) next to your new laptop. 
   Change its price. Save, and check the table again to verify the update.
5. To test DELETE: Click the yellow Soft Delete icon (archive box). Confirm it.
   The product will disappear from the active datatable list.


REQUIREMENT 2: Product/Service CRUDRestore datatable upload a single photo (10pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. First, test Restore: In the Datatables search box on /admin/products, 
   type the word "Trashed". 
2. The table will instantly show your soft-deleted "Simulation Laptop". 
3. Click the green Recover icon (circular arrow) next to it. 
4. The product badge will change back to "Active", proving CRUDRestore works!
5. To test single photo upload: Click the Edit icon on the laptop again. 
   Under "Image" (the main single image input), select a picture from your 
   computer and click Save. 
6. Return to the datatable. Look at the "Image" column for your laptop; 
   you will now see the thumbnail you just uploaded.


REQUIREMENT 3: Product/Service CRUDRestore datatable upload/view multiple photos (15pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Edit the "Simulation Laptop" product again.
2. Scroll down specifically to the "Gallery Images" file input. 
3. Because this relies on multiple uploads, select 3 or 4 different pictures 
   at the same time from your computer folder, and click Save.
4. Back on the Datatable, click the white View icon (eye) next to your product.
5. You will be taken to the Product Information page. 
6. On the left side, you will see a fully interactive CSS image gallery that 
   lets you click through all the multiple photos you just uploaded.


REQUIREMENT 4: Product/Service CRUDRestore datatable upload/view multiple photos, import products on excel worksheet (20pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open Excel or Google Sheets on your computer to create a test file.
2. In Row 1, type the EXACT database column headers:
   A1: name
   B1: price
   C1: stock_qty
   D1: image_url
   E1: category_id
   F1: brand_id
3. In Row 2, fill in some fake test data:
   A2: Excel Imported Laptop
   B2: 45000
   C2: 25
   D2: https://example.com/laptop.jpg  (Or a local path)
   E2: 1
   F2: 1
4. Save the file to your computer as an Excel Workbook (.xlsx).
5. Head back to your datatable at /admin/products.
6. At the top of the page under "Product Import", click "Choose File" and 
   find the .xlsx file you just saved.
7. Click the blue "Import Excel File" button.
8. The page will reload. Scroll down to your datatable, and you will instantly 
   see "Excel Imported Laptop" fully populated into your database!

MP2 USER CRUD - SIMULATION GUIDE (20 PTS COMPLETE TIER)
======================================================================

Below are the step-by-step instructions on how to perfectly simulate and 
test all four rubric requirements in your application for the "User CRUD" module.

REQUIREMENT 1: User Registration with upload photo (5pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Make sure you are completely logged out of the application.
2. Go to the homepage (/) and click the "Register" button in the top right.
3. Fill in your Name, Email, Contact Number, and Password.
4. Focus on the new "Profile Photo" field. Click it and select an image from your computer to upload.
5. Check the Terms agreement and click "Create Account".
6. The system will create your user, save the uploaded image securely, and assign you a Customer role.

REQUIREMENT 2: User update profile and upload photo. (5pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. If you just registered, you can click your account dropdown and go to "My Profile". 
2. OR, if you are logged in as an Admin: Go to the Admin dashboard, click on your profile name at the top right, and select "Profile".
3. In the "Account Information" section, you will see your current Profile Photo thumbnail. 
4. Click the "Choose File" input next to the thumbnail, pick a new image, and click "Save Profile". 
5. The webpage will reload and instantly display your newly updated photo.

REQUIREMENT 3: list users on a datatable. (5pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Log in to your Admin account.
2. Go to the "Users" tab in the admin sidebar (/admin/user).
3. The user list is now fully powered by jQuery DataTables!
4. Type any name or email in the DataTables "Search" box (top right of the table) to see instant client-side filtering. 
5. Change the "Show N entries" dropdown (top left of the table) to test dynamic pagination.

REQUIREMENT 4: admin can set the status of a user to active/inactive. update role of user. (5pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. While still on the Admin User List (/admin/user), find any normal customer account.
2. To test Status (Active/Inactive): Click the yellow "Deactivate" button (user-minus icon) on their row. 
3. The database will set is_active to false. Their status badge will instantly change to "Inactive", and they will be blocked from logging in. You can click the green "Activate" button to restore them.
4. To test Role Update: Click the blue "Edit" button (pencil icon) on any user.
5. In the User Edit form, locate the "Role" dropdown. Change it from "Customer" to "Admin" (or vice versa) and hit Save.
6. The user's permissions and access level have now been successfully upgraded/downgraded!

MP3 AUTHENTICATION & SECURITY - SIMULATION GUIDE (20 PTS COMPLETE TIER)
======================================================================

Below are the step-by-step instructions on how to perfectly simulate and 
test all four rubric requirements in your application for the "Authentication" module.

REQUIREMENT 1: Authentication & Email Verification After Registration
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Completely log out of any active account.
2. Go to the "Register" page and create a brand new test account with a 
   real or Mailtrap-accessible email address.
3. Upon clicking Register, you will NOT be logged in immediately. Instead, 
   you will be redirected to the "Verify Email" notice page.
4. Check your email inbox. You will see a securely generated Laravel email 
   verification link.
5. Click the "Verify Email Address" button in the email.
6. The system will successfully verify your email and redirect you to the 
   homepage.

REQUIREMENT 2: Only Verified Users Can Login
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Log out again. 
2. Register another brand new account, but this time, DO NOT verify the email.
3. Try to log in with the new account's credentials.
4. The system will reject the login and display an error message stating: 
   "Please verify your email address before logging in."
5. Only after clicking the email verification link will you be allowed to log in!

REQUIREMENT 3: Admin Routes Protected From Unauthenticated Users
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Make sure you are completely logged out.
2. Manually type an admin URL into your browser address bar (e.g. /admin/dashboard 
   or /admin/products).
3. The system will intercept the request and redirect you back to the homepage 
   with an error: "Please login first to access the admin dashboard."

REQUIREMENT 4: Admin Routes Protected From Unauthorized Users (Not Admin Role)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Log in using a standard "Customer" account.
2. Manually type an admin URL into your browser address bar (e.g. /admin/dashboard).
3. The system's custom middleware will intercept the request, realize you do not 
   have Admin privileges, and kick you back to the homepage with a bright red 
   error: "Unauthorized access to admin dashboard."
4. Furthermore, specific critical management routes (like User Management) are 
   strictly locked even from "Inventory Managers", ensuring unbreakable security.

test the MP6 advanced filtering rubric requirements in your application.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

4. Check a single box, such as "Budget (₱0 - ₱30,000)".

   of ₱30,000 or below.

   and click "Apply" to see a combined price scope.

4. Under "Brand", check a specific brand, such as "ASUS".
5. Under "Price Range", check "Premium (₱70,000 - ₱150,000)".
6. Click "Apply".
7. Observe that the system flawlessly combines text search, category, brand, 
   and price. The URL will reflect all these query parameters perfectly

Below are the step-by-step instructions on how to perfectly simulate and 
test the MP7 Charts rubric requirements in your application.

REQUIREMENT 1: Yearly Sales Chart (8pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Log in as an Administrator.
2. Navigate to the left sidebar and click "Analytics Charts" under the 
   "Reports" section.
3. Look at the bottom-right chart titled "Yearly Sales". 
4. On the top-right corner of that card, use the Year input stepper to pick 
   any target year (e.g., 2025), and click "Set Year".
5. This beautiful line chart fetches all delivered order revenue from 
   the database, extracts the year from your selection, groups it 
   natively by month, and displays it. Hover over the nodes to see exact monthly totals.

REQUIREMENT 2: Bar Chart with Date Picker & Product Pie Chart (15pts)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. On the same "Analytics Charts" page, look at the top section.

   (e.g., set start date to a few months ago) and click "Apply Filters".
3. The page reloads and instantly regenerates the two dynamic charts:
   - "Sales Revenue": A robust Bar Chart plotting everyday revenue 
     within that exact date range.
   - "Product Sales Contribution": A colorful Pie/Doughnut Chart 
     calculating the exact percentage (and raw ₱ amount) of total 
     sales contributed by every individual product sold in that timeframe.
4. Hover over the Bar Chart columns or Pie Chart slices to see the 
   dynamic JavaScript tooltips rendering the precise data payload.
